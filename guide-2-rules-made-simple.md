# Lacrosse Rules Made Simple
## An Illustrated Guide for Parents & New Players

*Created with Coach Zach — former professional lacrosse player, founder of elite clubs in San Diego & Central Texas, father of two state champion sons.*

---

> **From Coach Zach:**
> "Every season, I see parents on the sideline looking genuinely lost when the ref blows the whistle. That's not their fault — lacrosse has a lot going on. This guide changes that. By the time you finish, you'll understand more than most parents who've been watching for years."

## Part 1: The Field — What You're Looking At

```
|                                                            |
|  - - - - - - - - - CENTER LINE - - - - - - - - - - - - -  |
|           [ CENTER CIRCLE ] ←  face-offs happen here      |
|                                                            |
|  DEFENSE ZONE                                              |

|                                                            |
|         [  GOAL CREASE  ]                                  |
|              [ G O A L ]                                   |
|                                                            |
|============================================================|
```

**Field Size (Men's/Boys'):** 110 yards long × 60 yards wide
**Field Size (Women's/Girls'):** Similar, with different crease rules

### Key Zones Explained

| Zone | What It Is |
|------|-----------|
| **Attack Zone** | The area your team attacks. Most of your offense happens here. |
| **Defense Zone** | The area in front of your own goal. Your defenders protect this. |
| **Midfield** | The middle third. Your midfielders operate through this area. |
| **Crease** | The circle around each goal. Special rules apply here. |
| **Goal Line Extended (GLE)** | An imaginary line extending from post to post. Important for behind-the-goal plays. |

## Part 2: Positions — Who Does What

### Men's/Boys' Lacrosse (10 players per team on field)

#### ⚔️ ATTACK (3 players)
The scorers. They spend most of their time in the offensive zone and are responsible for generating goals. They typically do NOT come back to defend.

*What to watch:* Movement off-ball, picks, shooting angles, dodges.

---

#### 🔄 MIDFIELDERS / MIDS (3 players)
The engine of the team. Midfielders run the full length of the field — they attack AND defend. This is the most physically demanding position.

*What to watch:* Transition runs (sprinting from one end to the other after a turnover), ground ball pickups, shooting on the run.

**Coach Zach's Note:** "The midfield battle usually decides who wins the game. Watch how fast these guys can go coast to coast."

---

#### 🛡️ DEFENSE (3 players)
Their job is to protect the goal and take the ball away. Defenders use longer sticks (up to 6 feet) to check and disrupt opponents.

*What to watch:* Body positioning, stick checks, communication with the goalie.

---

#### 🥅 GOALIE (1 player)
The last line of defense. The goalie also directs the entire defense — they can see the whole field. After a save, the goalie is a quarterback distributing the ball.

*What to watch:* Footwork, stick saves, outlet passes to start the fast break.

---

### Women's/Girls' Lacrosse (12 players per team on field)

Women's lacrosse has fewer contact rules and different positions:

| Position | Role |
|----------|------|
| **Attack** (including center) | Score goals, control possession |
| **Midfield** | Connect attack and defense |
| **Defense** (including goalkeeper) | Protect goal, clear ball |

**Key Difference:** Women's lacrosse is non-contact (or minimal contact). Stick and body checks are much more restricted than the men's game.

## Part 3: How the Game Flows

### Game Structure

| Level | Quarters | Quarter Length |
|-------|----------|---------------|
| Youth (U9–U12) | 4 quarters | 8–10 minutes |
| Youth (U13–U14) | 4 quarters | 10–12 minutes |
| High School | 4 quarters | 12 minutes |
| College | 4 quarters | 15 minutes |

**Halftime** is usually 10 minutes. **Overtime** (if tied): sudden death — first goal wins.

---

### How Possession Starts: The Face-Off

The game starts and restarts after each goal with a **face-off**.

**How it works:**
1. One midfielder from each team crouches at center field, sticks on the ground
2. The referee places the ball between the two stick heads
3. Ref blows the whistle → both players fight for possession
4. The winner scoops or clamps the ball and runs or passes to teammates

**What to watch:** The clamp (pressing the stick head over the ball), the rake (sweeping the ball sideways), and the quick outlet pass.

*Women's lacrosse uses a "draw" instead of a face-off — two players stand upright, sticks vertical, and push them apart on the whistle.*

---

### How the Ball Moves

Players move the ball by:
1. **Running** with the ball in their stick (cradling)
2. **Passing** — throwing to a teammate
3. **Catching** — receiving a pass
4. **Shooting** — throwing at the goal to score

There's no dribbling like basketball. The ball stays in the stick's mesh pocket. Players "cradle" (rock the stick back and forth) to keep the ball from falling out while running.

---

### Scoring

A goal counts when the ball completely crosses the goal line between the posts and under the crossbar.

**1 point per goal.** No 2-pointers, no 3-pointers. Simple.

The team with the most goals at the end of 4 quarters wins.

## Part 4: Common Penalties — Plain English

### 🔴 PERSONAL FOULS (More Serious — Result in Penalty Time)

The offending player sits off the field for **1–3 minutes** while their team plays one man down.

---

#### Cross-Check
**What it is:** Hitting an opponent with the *handle* of the stick (the part between your hands), rather than a legal body or stick check.
**What you'll see:** Player drives the middle of the stick into an opponent's body or stick.
**The call:** "Cross-check, #14, 1 minute."

---

#### Slashing
**What it is:** A vicious stick check that hits a player in a dangerous area — usually the helmet, neck, or hands with excessive force.
**What you'll see:** Uncontrolled, wild swinging at an opponent.
**The call:** "Slash, #7, 1 minute."

---

#### Tripping
**What it is:** Using the stick or body to trip an opponent.

---

#### Unnecessary Roughness
**What it is:** Any violent or excessively aggressive contact beyond normal play.
**Coach Zach's Note:** "This is the ref's catch-all for 'that went too far.' It's judgment-based."

---

#### Illegal Body Check
**What it is:** Hitting a player who doesn't have the ball, or a hit from behind, or a hit to the head.

---

### 🟡 TECHNICAL FOULS (Less Serious — Lose Possession or 30-Second Penalty)

---

#### Holding
**What it is:** Using your free hand to hold an opponent's body or stick.

---

#### Pushing
**What it is:** Using hands to push an opponent, especially from behind.

---

#### Offsides (Men's)
**What it is:** Having fewer than 3 attackers in the offensive zone OR fewer than 3 defenders in the defensive zone at any time.
**Why it matters:** Teams must maintain these numbers to keep balance on the field.
**What you'll see:** A player runs past the center line when they shouldn't, or a defender follows their attack too far up the field.

---

#### Crease Violation
**What it is:** An offensive player stepping into the goal crease circle.
**What you'll see:** An attacker's foot touches the crease line while attempting a shot or pickup.
**The call:** Turnover. Defense gets the ball.

**Coach Zach's Note:** "This one confuses new parents the most. Watch the attacker's feet near the crease. Even if a goal is scored, if their foot was in the crease — no goal."

---

#### Warding Off
**What it is:** Using the free hand or arm to push away a defender's stick check.
**What you'll see:** Ball carrier extends their non-stick arm outward to block a check.

---

#### Stalling
**What it is:** Intentionally keeping the ball without trying to advance toward the goal.
**What happens:** Ref warns the team, then a "keep it in" rule applies — they must stay in the offensive zone.

## Part 5: Quick-Reference Glossary

| Term | What It Means |
|------|---------------|
| **Cradling** | Rocking the stick to keep the ball in the pocket while running |
| **Ground ball** | A loose ball on the ground — often results in a scramble |
| **Clear** | Moving the ball from your defensive zone to midfield or attack |
| **Ride** | Defensive pressure applied to prevent the opposing team from clearing |
| **Fast break** | A quick transition offense with a numbers advantage |
| **EMO** | Extra-Man Offense — playing with a man advantage during a penalty |
| **Man-down** | Playing with fewer players due to a penalty |
| **Feed** | A pass that sets up a shot or score |
| **Dodge** | A move to get past a defender (split dodge, roll dodge, etc.) |
| **Settle** | Calming possession down to run a set play |
| **Dump** | A short, safe pass when under pressure |
| **Pipe** | Hitting the post. "Off the pipe!" = ball hit the goalpost |
| **Cage** | The goal |
| **Rock / Ball** | The lacrosse ball |
| **Stick** | The lacrosse stick (also called a "twig" informally) |
| **D-pole** | The long defensive stick used by defenders |
| **LSM** | Long-Stick Midfielder — a defender using a long pole in midfield |

## Part 6: Bonus — Beginner Drills for Backyard Practice

These require only a stick, a ball, and a wall or rebounder. Perfect for a new player working on fundamentals.

---

### Drill 1: Wall Ball (The #1 Skill Builder)

**What:** Throw the ball against a wall or rebounder, catch the return.
**Time:** 10–15 minutes daily
**Start with:** 25 catches with dominant hand, 25 with non-dominant hand
**Progress to:** Throw with right, catch with left; throw with left, catch with right.

**Coach Zach's Note:** "Wall ball is the great equalizer. The players who do 15 minutes of wall ball daily will surpass kids with more natural talent within one season."

---

### Drill 2: Cradling Figure-8

**What:** Hold the stick in one hand, cradle (rock) it back and forth while walking around obstacles (cones, shoes, water bottles set in a line).
**Goal:** Ball never falls out. Eyes up, not watching the stick.
**Time:** 5 minutes

---

### Drill 3: Ground Balls

**What:** Roll a ball across the grass, scoop it up cleanly at full speed.
**Keys:** Bend your knees, bring the stick flat to the ground, scoop through the ball (don't poke at it).
**Reps:** 20–30 scoops

---

### Drill 4: Shooting on a Goal (or Makeshift Target)

**What:** Mark a small goal with cones or tape on a wall. Shoot from 8–10 yards.
**Targets:** Aim for corners, not the middle.
**Reps:** 20 shots each session

---

### Drill 5: Catch While Moving

**What:** Two players (or parent + child) pass back and forth while jogging laterally.
**Goal:** Don't stop to catch. Keep moving. This replicates real game passing.
**Time:** 10 minutes

## Cheat Sheet: Take This to the Game

Print this and keep it in your pocket on game day.

```
SCORE: 1 pt per goal. Most goals wins.
QUARTERS: 4 quarters (length varies by age)
ON FIELD: 10 players each side (men's) / 12 (women's)
START/RESTART: Face-off at center (men's) / Draw (women's)

FOULS — PERSONAL (1-3 min penalty):
• Cross-check     • Slashing
• Tripping        • Unnecessary roughness
• Illegal body check

FOULS — TECHNICAL (turnover or 30 sec):
• Holding         • Pushing
• Offsides        • Crease violation
• Warding off     • Stalling

KEY RULE: No feet in the crease (offense)!
         Ball stays in pocket — no hands!
         Always 3 defenders, 3 attackers on their sides.
```
